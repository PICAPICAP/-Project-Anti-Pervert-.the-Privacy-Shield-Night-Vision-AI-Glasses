## 🚀 Core Features (Explained to Your Grandma)

We aren’t building a sci-fi optical cloaking device—we are simply utilizing clever physics and hardware to give you three killer features. Here is how they work, explained using everyday analogies:

### 1. One-Way Mirror X-Ray (The "Liar Liar" Detector)
*   **The Physics:** Physical "magic glass" that lets light travel in only one direction doesn't exist. One-way mirrors rely on a 50/50 half-silvered coating and massive lighting differences (a bright interrogation room vs. a pitch-black observation room).
*   **The Analogy:** It’s like trying to see inside a house during a bright sunny day. From the outside, the window acts as a mirror reflecting the sun. But if you cup your hands around your eyes and press against the glass, you kill the reflection and see right through. Our glasses do exactly that, but from 2 meters away without you looking like a suspicious weirdo.

### 2. Spy-Cam Hunter (The "Cat-Eye" Sniper)
*   **The Physics:** No matter how microscopic a hidden pinhole camera is (even 1mm wide), its lens *must* be exposed to capture light. When our glasses shoot an invisible beam into the lens, the light hits the camera's internal CMOS sensor and bounces straight back at a 180-degree angle. This is known as "retroreflection" or the "cat-eye effect."
*   **The Analogy:** Think of driving at night and seeing a stray cat on the road. The cat's eyes suddenly flash like bright neon bulbs under your car's headlights. Our glasses act as the headlight, turning every hidden camera in your hotel room into a glowing red beacon on your screen.

### 3. Ghost Protocol Night Vision (The "Invisible Flashlight")
*   **The Physics:** By physically removing the Infrared-Cut filter (IR-Cut) from our custom camera sensor, the glasses can see Near-Infrared (NIR) light that is completely invisible to human eyes. 
*   **The Analogy:** Imagine navigating a pitch-black hotel room in total darkness. You turn on a giant flashlight, but the light wave is outside the human spectrum. To anyone else, you are sitting in pitch darkness; to your AI glasses, the room is illuminated as bright as high noon.

*   **生活比喻：** 這就像在大白天你想看進別人家車窗裡一樣。從外面看，車窗就像一面鏡子反射太陽光；但如果你用雙手摀住眼睛緊貼車窗，切斷外面的反射光，你就能瞬間看穿車內。我們的眼鏡就是幫你做這件事，但讓你在 2 公尺外的安全距離就能看穿，不需要整個人貼在玻璃上像個可疑的變態。

*   **生活比喻：** 想想你大晚上開車時，路邊野貓的眼睛在你的車大燈照射下，會突然像霓虹燈一樣發出耀眼的光芒。我們的眼鏡就是那個車大燈，能把飯店房間裡所有隱藏的針孔鏡頭，在你的螢幕上直接變成一個個發亮的大紅點。

### 3. 鬼影特攻夜視鏡（「隱形的手電筒」）
*   **物理原理：** 我們在硬體上移除了相機感測器前的「紅外線濾鏡（IR-Cut）」，讓這顆鏡頭能接收大自然中微弱的「近紅外線（NIR）」，這類光線是人類肉眼完全看不見的。
*   **生活比喻：** 想像你半夜在全黑的飯店房間裡醒來，你打開了一支巨大無比的手電筒，但這支手電筒發出的光波超出人類肉眼範圍。在別人眼裡，你依然坐在伸手不見五指的漆黑中；但在你的 AI 眼鏡鏡頭裡，整個房間亮得就像正中午一樣。

We refuse to over-engineer. To keep the glasses lightweight (<45g), cost-effective, and safe from overheating on your face, Project Anti-Pervert adopts a **Split Processing Architecture**. The glasses function as a dumb edge-node that only captures raw sensor data, while your smartphone’s powerful NPU handles the heavy sensor fusion math via high-bandwidth wireless protocols.

### 1. The Metric Anchor: Micro Single-Point dToF Sensor
*   **The Hardware:** A millimeter-scale Direct Time-of-Flight (dToF) sensor (e.g., STMicroelectronics FlightSense series). It weighs practically nothing and costs a mere few dollars.
*   **The Job:** It shoots a continuous, safe infrared laser pulse to measure the *exact physical distance* of whatever you are looking at in centimeters. This acts as our uncompromising real-world ruler, solving the "scale ambiguity" nightmare that plagues pure-camera AI glasses.

### 2. The Optical Sensor: No-IR Cut Day/Night HDR Camera
*   **The Hardware:** A custom high-dynamic-range (HDR) camera module with its physical Infrared-Cut (IR-Cut) filter completely removed from the assembly line.
*   **The Job:** Standard smart glasses filter out infrared light to make photos look pretty. We keep it. This sensor captures both visible light and near-infrared (NIR) spectra simultaneously, ensuring it can record in raw pitch-black darkness and catch hidden camera IR illuminators.

### 3. The Active Igniter: Micro 940nm Infrared/Laser Strobe
*   **The Hardware:** An array of ultra-miniature 940nm vertical-cavity surface-emitting lasers (VCSEL) or high-efficiency IR LEDs embedded in the frame.
*   **The Job:** Dual-purpose hardware. In scanning mode, it strobes rapidly to trigger the retroreflective "cat-eye" bounce from hidden camera lenses. In night mode, it stays on, acting as the invisible flashlight for digital night vision.

### 4. The Signal Interceptor: Lightweight RF Sniffer Chip
*   **The Hardware:** A low-cost Radio Frequency (RF) scanning chip operating alongside a standard high-speed wireless communication module.
*   **The Job:** It passively sniffs the local airwaves for hidden device data broadcasts. It tracks Received Signal Strength Indication (RSSI) spikes, passing the raw wireless telemetry to the app for physical cross-referencing.

## 🧠 The Magic Sauce: Multi-Sensor Fusion Algorithms

Since we aren’t putting an industrial supercomputer on your nose, the heavy lifting happens inside our custom software pipelines on the smartphone app. Here is how Gemini’s algorithmic logic processes the raw sensor feed to protect your privacy:

### 1. Spatial Conflict Fusion Network (One-Way Mirror Detection)
This model calculates whether the glass in front of you is a harmless mirror or a suspicious observation window by combining computer vision and laser telemetry:
*   **Step A (Passive CV Estimation):** The app takes the raw HDR video frame and passes it through a Monocular Depth Estimation network. Based on visual cues like edge contrast and light occlusion, the AI determines: *"Visually, this mirror is exactly 1.5 meters away."*
*   **Step B (Active Laser Anchor):** Simultaneously, the micro dToF sensor shoots a physical laser pulse through the glass. Since it’s a one-way mirror, the light passes through the silvered layer and hits the solid back wall of the secret room before bouncing back. The dToF measures the real metric distance: *"Physically, the barrier is 3.5 meters away."*
*   **Step C (Conflict Reconciliation):** The Fusion Network instantly compares the two values. When it detects a spatial delta (1.5m visual vs. 3.5m physical), the algorithm triggers a 100% certain **One-Way Mirror Alert**.

### 2. Retroreflective Cat-Eye Filtering (Hidden Camera Tracker)
To spot hidden pinhole lenses without false positives from shiny door handles or faucets, the algorithm uses a temporal flash比對 (strobe-comparison) method:
*   **Strobe Synchronization:** The app synchronizes the camera shutter with the 940nm laser strobe on the frame. It captures alternating frames: Frame A (Laser On) and Frame B (Laser Off).
*   **Differential Frame Substraction:** The AI subtracts Frame B from Frame A. Static reflections from metal objects cancel out. However, a true hidden camera lens—which exhibits perfect 180-degree retroreflection—will show up as a violent, pulsating pixel spike.
*   **Feature Verification:** The algorithm checks the shape and light gradient of the pulsating spot against a known database of optical lens signatures, completely eliminating false alarms.

### 3. Electro-Visual Spatial Co-Location (Signal Tracking)
When the RF sniffer chip picks up suspicious data packet transmissions, the algorithm maps the wireless world onto your physical vision:
*   **RSSI Delta Computation:** As you move your head, the app tracks changes in the wireless Received Signal Strength Indication (RSSI).
*   **Triangulation:** By combining the RSSI signal curve with the head-tracking IMU data and dToF depth tracking, the AI calculates the likely origin coordinates of the transmitting source.
*   **AR Target Overlay:** The app outputs an AR bounding box overlay onto the camera view, telling you: *"Warning! Suspicious wireless data stream detected coming from this smoke detector."*

## 🧠 核心靈魂：多感測器融合演算法

既然我們沒有在你的鼻樑上放一台工業級超級電腦，所有的重頭戲都發生在手機隨身 App 的自訂軟體管道中。以下是 Gemini 演算法邏輯如何處理原始感測器數據流，進而保護你的穩私：

### 1. 空間衝突融合網路（單面鏡偵測）
該模型結合了計算視覺與雷射遙測，用來計算你面前的玻璃到底是普通的鏡子還是可疑的觀察窗：
*   **步驟 A（被動視覺估算）：** App 接收原始 HDR 影像幀，並將其傳入「單鏡頭深度估算網路」。根據邊緣對比度、光線遮蔽等視覺線索，AI 判定：*「在視覺上，這面鏡子距離你正好 1.5 公尺。」*
*   **步驟 B（主動雷射錨點）：** 與此同時，微型 dToF 感測器向前方射出物理雷射脈衝。因為它是單面鏡，光線會穿透半反射層，打到密室後方的實心牆壁後折返。dToF 測量出真實的公制距離：*「在物理上，障礙物距離你有 3.5 公尺。」*
*   **步驟 C（衝突調停機制）：** 融合網路會瞬間比對這兩個數值。當它偵測到嚴重的空間差（視覺 1.5m vs 物理 3.5m）時，演算法便會觸發 100% 準確的**單面鏡警報**。

### 2. 逆向貓眼特徵過濾（針孔相機追蹤）
為了能精準揪出隱蔽的針孔鏡頭，同時不把亮晶晶的門把或水龍頭誤判為偷拍源，演算法採用了「時序頻閃比對法」：
*   **頻閃同步化：** App 會將相機快門與鏡框上的 940nm 雷射頻閃進行精準同步。它會交替捕捉兩個畫面：畫面 A（雷射開啟）與畫面 B（雷射關閉）。
*   **差分圖像相減：** AI 會將畫面 A 減去畫面 B。來自普通金屬物體的靜態反光會被直接抵消。然而，真正的隱藏相機鏡頭因為具備完美的 180 度逆向反射特性，會在畫面上呈現出一個劇烈、閃爍的像素峰值。

## 🗺️ Roadmap: Software First (Because Hardware Is Expensive)

We are tech laymen, which means we do not have tens of millions of dollars to burning away on advanced optical manufacturing lines and injection molding on day one. We believe in being lean. 

Before we ever manufacture a single pair of frames, our roadmap dictates a **Software-First Validation Strategy**. We will use existing high-end smartphones as our sandbox to prove Gemini’s algorithmic vision works before freezing the hardware design.

### Phase 1: The Smartphone Sandbox (Current PoC)
*   **The Target Platform:** iPhones equipped with rear LiDAR sensors (iPhone 12 Pro through current generations).
*   **The Mission:** We write the smartphone companion app first. The iPhone’s rear LiDAR acts as our proxy dToF metric anchor, and the main camera acts as our HDR feed. 
*   **The Experiment:** We place an iPhone Pro on a tripod in front of a one-way mirror in a simulated interrogation-room setup. If our Spatial Conflict algorithm can consistently flag the 1.5m visual vs 3.5m physical contradiction using the phone's native hardware, the software is validated.

### Phase 2: Embedded Hardware Mapping & Splitting
*   **The Mission:** Extract the validated algorithm code from the phone sandbox and write the micro-API. 
*   **The Experiment:** Wire a discrete $2 dToF breakout board and an IR-capable webcam sensor to a development board, interface it with the mobile app via a high-speed wireless stream, and optimize latency.

### Phase 3: Miniature Tooling & Mass Mockery
*   **The Mission:** Shrink the development board components into a flexible PCB that fits inside an injection-molded acetate eyeglass frame weighing under 45 grams.
